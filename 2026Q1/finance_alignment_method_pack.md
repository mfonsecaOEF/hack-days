# Finance Alignment Method Pack

## Chosen Use Case
- Country: Brazil
- Project concept: Community-led flood resilience green corridors in Porto Alegre peripheries
- Project type: Adaptation
- Why this case: It fits OEF's existing Brazil, NbS, and project-preparation context and is a strong match for your interest in governance, SDGs, NDCs, and climate finance.

## Evidence Sheet
- OEF context: CityCatalyst is framed as a platform that helps cities move from assessment to finance-ready projects, and OEF already has active NbS project-preparation work tied to Porto Alegre and Brazil.
- NDC evidence: Brazil's Second NDC says implementation will rely on a national Climate Plan including a National Adaptation Strategy, sixteen adaptation plans, just transition, means of implementation, and participatory climate governance across levels of government.
- Finance evidence: BNDES already has a resilient and sustainable urban development line under Fundo Clima, and Brazil's Ministry of Finance has launched the Brazil Climate and Ecological Transformation Investment Platform to connect plans with investable pipelines.
- SDG evidence: UNDP's SDG budget-tagging brief supports linking projects to national and subnational budget decisions rather than relying only on broad narrative alignment.
- Governance evidence: UNICEF's climate declaration explicitly supports meaningful youth participation in NDCs, adaptation plans, and climate decision-making, which gives you a strong rationale for adding youth governance to the card.
- GFLAC evidence: GFLAC's sustainable finance framing is useful as a country-context lens because it tracks whether public revenues and expenditures are aligned with low-carbon and climate-resilient development, while also highlighting continuing carbon-intensive dependence.

## Governance Checklist
- Is the project designed with community participation before site selection and design are finalized?
- Is there at least one defined civil society or community-based organization role beyond consultation?
- Is there an explicit youth governance mechanism with real responsibilities, not just outreach language?
- Is there a plan for stewardship, maintenance, and accountability after capital works are completed?
- Does the project definition explain how benefits will reach high-vulnerability or historically underserved neighborhoods?

## Scoring Rubric
| Category | 0-1 | 2-3 | 4-5 |
|---|---|---|---|
| SDG alignment | Weak or generic SDG references | Some plausible SDG links, limited specificity | Clear links to 3+ SDGs with project-specific justification |
| NDC / policy alignment | No clear policy fit | Partial fit to broad climate goals | Direct fit to named NDC or national policy priorities |
| Finance alignment | No funding logic | Some finance relevance, weak instrument fit | Clear fit to at least one public, blended, or climate-finance pathway |
| Governance readiness | Participation absent or symbolic | Participation mentioned but vague | Concrete community and civil society roles are built into implementation |
| Youth/community inclusion | No explicit inclusion structure | Inclusion language but no mechanism | Named youth/community governance mechanism with responsibilities |

## Overall Funding Narrative Rule
- Weak: 0-10 total points
- Medium: 11-18 total points
- Strong: 19-25 total points

## How To Reuse This Today
- Step 1: Swap in a different country and project concept.
- Step 2: Pull the current NDC and one or two finance-policy signals.
- Step 3: Re-score the five categories.
- Step 4: Keep the card to one screen and only include claims you can source.
