# Problem Statement: Finance Alignment Card

## Title
Bridging Project Preparation, National Commitments, and Inclusive Governance for Climate Finance

## Context
OpenEarth Foundation is already building tools that help cities move from climate assessment to finance-ready projects. In OEF's current direction, this includes work on CityCatalyst, project preparation, and nature-based solutions in Brazil and Porto Alegre.

At the same time, sustainable finance conversations often start from a different angle: countries define climate and development commitments, map priorities through instruments such as NDCs and SDG-linked budgeting, and then use those commitments to justify access to funding. This creates an important question for OEF: even when a project is technically strong, are we making it easy to show that the project is also aligned with national commitments, public-finance priorities, and the governance expectations that funders increasingly care about?

## Core Problem
Today, there is a gap between:
- OEF's strength in helping cities define and prepare projects, and
- the policy, finance, and governance framing often needed to make those projects compelling to funders.

A project may be climate-relevant and locally valuable, but still remain hard to finance if we cannot clearly answer:
- Which SDGs does it support?
- How does it align with the country's NDC or national climate policies?
- Is there evidence that this type of project fits existing public-finance or climate-finance priorities?
- Does the project include credible participation by communities, civil society, and youth?

## Why This Matters
This matters because climate finance is not only about technical project quality. It is also about policy fit, institutional legitimacy, and implementation readiness.

For OEF, adding this layer could strengthen the transition from action prioritization to finance-ready projects by making it easier to explain why a project is:
- strategically aligned,
- more likely to attract funding,
- and better designed for equitable implementation.

## Personal Motivation and Lens
This hack-day project is also shaped by my current learning in a sustainable finance course. In the course, a recurring theme is that funding access is often linked to how well projects connect to broader national and international frameworks, especially:
- the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs),
- Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs),
- national climate and sector policies,
- and public budget or investment priorities.

That perspective led me to ask whether OEF is already considering these policy and finance alignment layers in its project-preparation work, and whether we could prototype a simple way to bring them into the workflow.

Another important input from my own perspective is that implementation quality depends on governance. It is not enough to prepare projects only for institutions and financiers; it is also very important to include communities and civil society in implementation, especially through meaningful youth governance.

## How the Knowledge MCP Is Being Used
The OEF Knowledge MCP is a key part of this project because it helps ground the idea in OEF's real context rather than in a generic climate-finance concept.

For this prototype, the Knowledge MCP was used to:
- identify what OEF already has on project preparation and finance-ready projects,
- confirm that CityCatalyst is framed as a tool that helps cities progress from assessment to finance-ready projects,
- locate existing Brazil- and Porto Alegre-related project-preparation context,
- connect the idea to OEF's NbS, Journey Navigator, and BPJP work,
- and distinguish between what OEF already documents well and what still appears to be missing.

In practice, this means the prototype is not starting from zero. It is building on top of OEF's existing product and program logic, while adding a new alignment layer that may not yet be explicit in the current workflow.

## Proposed Response
The proposed response is a lightweight prototype called the **Finance Alignment Card**.

The card is a one-page artifact that evaluates a project across five dimensions:
- SDG alignment
- NDC / national policy alignment
- finance context
- governance readiness
- youth and community inclusion

Its purpose is to help OEF test whether a simple, reusable format can make project concepts more legible to funders and more accountable to communities.

## Guiding Question
How might OEF help turn a city climate project into a finance-ready concept that is not only technically sound, but also clearly aligned with SDGs, national climate commitments, public-finance signals, and inclusive governance?

## Expected Value
If useful, this prototype could become:
- a framing layer inside project preparation workflows,
- a reusable template for early-stage concept development,
- or an input into future CityCatalyst and NbS project-preparation tools.

Even as a hack-day prototype, it is valuable because it tests whether stronger policy, finance, and governance framing can improve how OEF positions projects for funding.
